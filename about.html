About the Journal

Mission

The Journal of Undergraduate Horticultural Research (JUHR) is an undergraduate scholarly journal dedicated to preserving and disseminating undergraduate research, reviews, hypotheses, perspectives, and scientific ideas in horticultural science.

JUHR provides an academic platform for undergraduate students to develop and communicate scientific ideas in horticultural science. The journal emphasizes intellectual curiosity, critical reasoning, and scholarly communication. Experimental studies are welcome, but strong literature-based reviews, hypotheses, perspectives, and conceptual models are equally valued.

Scope

JUHR publishes undergraduate scholarship across the breadth of horticultural science, including but not limited to:

  • Fruit, vegetable, and ornamental crop production
  • Plant physiology, propagation, and breeding
  • Controlled-environment agriculture and plant factories
  • Postharvest biology and technology
  • Soil, water, and nutrient management
  • Urban horticulture and landscape management
  • Climate change and environmental interactions
  • Data science, modeling, and AI applications in horticulture

Journal Philosophy

JUHR is designed as an undergraduate academic archive. The journal exists to preserve valuable student ideas, hypotheses, reviews, and research outputs that might otherwise disappear after coursework or capstone projects end.

JUHR does not aim to imitate high-impact professional journals. Instead, it provides undergraduate students with a structured scholarly platform to practice scientific writing, argumentation, literature synthesis, and research communication. The journal values strong questions, sound logic, and persuasive evidence over experimental scale or funding level.

Why Undergraduate Research Matters

Undergraduate students generate genuine scientific insight in coursework, capstone projects, laboratory activities, field studies, and independent inquiry. Most of that work is never recorded in a citable, public form. By giving this work a durable home with formal peer review, JUHR helps students:

  • Experience the full scholarly publishing cycle, from submission to revision to publication
  • Build a citable academic record before graduate study or employment
  • Practice scientific reasoning and persuasive scholarly writing
  • Contribute ideas and data that future students can build on

What We Value

JUHR explicitly values questions, hypotheses, literature synthesis, and logical reasoning. A well-argued hypothesis paper or a careful literature review is judged on the same scholarly standards as an experimental study: the importance and clarity of the question, the quality of the reasoning, and the strength of the evidence.
