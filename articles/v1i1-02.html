Effect of LED Light Quality on Lettuce Seedling Growth in a Small-Scale Plant Factory

Choi M.1, Kang D.1, and Chung S. W.1

1 Department of Horticulture, Chonnam National University, Gwangju, Republic of Korea

Received: pending · Accepted: pending · Published: 2026
JUHR 1(1): 9–15 · DOI: pending

Abstract

[Sample placeholder abstract.] Light quality strongly influences seedling morphology and quality in controlled-environment agriculture. In this study, lettuce (Lactuca sativa L.) seedlings were grown under three LED treatments (red, blue, and a red:blue combination) in a small-scale plant factory module. We measured hypocotyl length, leaf area, fresh and dry mass, and chlorophyll content over three weeks. The combined red:blue treatment produced the most compact seedlings with the highest dry mass, while monochromatic red light increased hypocotyl elongation. These results provide practical guidance for low-cost seedling production systems operated by students.

Keywords: lettuce, LED, light quality, plant factory, seedling quality
