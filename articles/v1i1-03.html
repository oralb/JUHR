Root-Zone Temperature as a Hidden Driver of Flowering Time in Strawberry: A Hypothesis

Jung Y.1 and Han S.1

1 Department of Horticulture, Chonnam National University, Gwangju, Republic of Korea

Received: pending · Accepted: pending · Published: 2026
JUHR 1(1): 16–20 · DOI: pending

Abstract

[Sample placeholder abstract.] Flowering time in strawberry (Fragaria × ananassa) is conventionally explained by air temperature and photoperiod. Drawing on published evidence from soilless cultivation, root physiology, and hormone signaling, we hypothesize that root-zone temperature acts as an additional, partially independent regulator of floral induction. We outline the proposed mechanism, identify testable predictions, and describe a simple experimental design using substrate heating and cooling that is feasible within an undergraduate research project.

Keywords: strawberry, flowering, root-zone temperature, floral induction, hypothesis
