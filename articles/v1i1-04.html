AI-Assisted Plant Phenotyping in Undergraduate Horticulture Education: A Perspective

Lim G.1

1 Department of Horticulture, Chonnam National University, Gwangju, Republic of Korea

Received: pending · Accepted: pending · Published: 2026
JUHR 1(1): 21–25 · DOI: pending

Abstract

[Sample placeholder abstract.] Image-based phenotyping powered by machine learning is rapidly becoming a standard tool in plant science, yet it remains rare in undergraduate horticulture curricula. This perspective argues that accessible AI tools — smartphone imaging, open-source models, and no-code analysis platforms — can let undergraduates collect and analyze phenotypic data at a quality once limited to specialized laboratories. We discuss opportunities, practical barriers, and the skills students should learn to use these tools critically and responsibly.

Keywords: phenotyping, machine learning, education, image analysis, undergraduate research
