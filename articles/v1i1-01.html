Climate Change and Apple Production: A Review

Kim J.1, Lee H.1, and Park S.1

1 Department of Horticulture, Chonnam National University, Gwangju, Republic of Korea

Received: pending · Accepted: pending · Published: 2026
JUHR 1(1): 1–8 · DOI: pending

Abstract

[Sample placeholder abstract.] Rising temperatures, shifting precipitation patterns, and increasingly frequent extreme weather events are altering the conditions under which apples are grown worldwide. This review synthesizes recent literature on the effects of climate change on apple phenology, fruit quality, pest and disease pressure, and suitable production regions. We discuss adaptation strategies including cultivar selection, orchard microclimate management, and shifts in production zones, and we identify open questions suitable for undergraduate research.

Keywords: apple, climate change, phenology, fruit quality, adaptation
