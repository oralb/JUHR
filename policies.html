Publication Policies

Peer Review Policy

All submissions are evaluated by faculty reviewers. The Editor-in-Chief assigns each manuscript to reviewers with relevant expertise and makes the final editorial decision.

Decision categories:

  1. Accept — the manuscript is accepted for publication, possibly with minor editorial corrections.
  2. Minor Revision — the manuscript requires limited changes before acceptance.
  3. Major Revision — the manuscript requires substantial changes and will be re-reviewed.
  4. Reject — the manuscript is not suitable for publication in JUHR.

Manuscripts are evaluated against five criteria:

  • Importance and clarity of the research question
  • Logical reasoning
  • Quality and relevance of evidence
  • Discussion and critical interpretation
  • Writing clarity and scholarly structure

Reviewers assess the quality of the scholarship, not the scale of the experiment or the level of funding. A well-reasoned hypothesis paper is held to the same standards of logic and evidence as an experimental study.

Open Access and Licensing

JUHR is a fully open-access journal. All articles are freely available to read and download immediately upon publication. There are no submission fees and no article processing charges.

Unless otherwise noted, articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) license. Authors retain copyright of their work and grant JUHR the right of first publication.

Publication Ethics

  • Originality: submissions must be the authors' own work and must not be published or under consideration elsewhere.
  • Plagiarism: plagiarism in any form, including self-plagiarism and uncredited reuse of text or figures, is grounds for rejection or retraction.
  • Data integrity: fabrication or falsification of data is a serious ethical violation and will result in rejection or retraction.
  • Authorship: all listed authors must have made a meaningful contribution and approved the final manuscript. The first author must be an undergraduate student.
  • Conflicts of interest: authors and reviewers must disclose any relationships that could bias the work or its evaluation.
  • Corrections and retractions: the journal will publish corrections or retract articles when significant errors or misconduct are identified after publication.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy

  • AI tools may be used responsibly to support literature searching, language editing, data analysis, code development, and idea refinement.
  • Every submission must include an AI use statement describing which tools were used and for what purpose, or stating that no AI tools were used.
  • The editorial office may use AI tools to assist with English translation of accepted manuscripts written in Korean. Every AI-assisted translation is returned to the authors for checking and is published only after the authors approve it; authors remain responsible for the accuracy of the published text.
  • AI systems may not be listed as authors and may not be cited as sources of scientific fact.
  • Authors are fully responsible for verifying the accuracy of any AI-assisted content, including references, which must be checked against original sources.

Archiving and Availability

JUHR operates as a durable undergraduate research archive. Published articles receive a stable citation (volume, issue, pages) and remain permanently and freely accessible on the journal website. DOI registration and ISSN assignment are planned as the journal develops.
