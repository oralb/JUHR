Author Guidelines

Please read these guidelines carefully before submitting. Manuscripts that do not follow the format may be returned to authors before review.

Authorship and Eligibility

  • The first author must be an undergraduate student (or have completed the work as an undergraduate).
  • Co-authors may include undergraduate students, graduate students, and faculty members.
  • A faculty advisor may serve as corresponding author.
  • Manuscripts may originate from coursework, capstone projects, undergraduate research programs, laboratory activities, field studies, or independent student inquiry.

Article Types

JUHR organizes submissions into three categories. Choose the category and sub-type that best fits your manuscript; if you are unsure, the editorial office will help you place it.

1. Research Articles

Manuscripts that present new data, observations, or tools.

Sub-typeDescription
Research ArticleOriginal research based on experiments, surveys, field observations, modeling, or data analysis.
Case StudyAnalysis of a specific horticultural case, farm, crop system, environmental event, or management problem.
Data PaperDescription of a useful dataset generated by undergraduate students.
Method NoteShort report describing an experimental, analytical, computational, or AI-assisted method.

2. Review Articles

Manuscripts that synthesize and evaluate existing literature.

Sub-typeDescription
Review ArticleNarrative or thematic review of scientific literature.
Systematic ReviewStructured literature review using explicit search, selection, and synthesis criteria.
Meta-analysisQuantitative synthesis of published data.

3. Scholarly & Theoretical Articles

Manuscripts that advance ideas, arguments, or frameworks grounded in published literature.

Sub-typeDescription
Hypothesis PaperA logically developed scientific hypothesis supported by literature.
PerspectiveScholarly viewpoint on emerging issues in horticultural science.
Model Concept PaperA conceptual framework for a biological, environmental, crop, or AI-based model.

Manuscript Format

  • File format: Microsoft Word (.docx) or equivalent editable format.
  • Language: English. Clear, concise scholarly writing.
  • Double-spaced text, 12-point font, numbered pages and lines.
  • Typical length: 3,000–6,000 words for full articles; 1,500–3,000 words for method notes and perspectives. Reasonable deviations are acceptable.

Title Page

Submit a separate title page containing:

  • Full article title and article type
  • All author names, affiliations, and email addresses
  • Identification of the undergraduate first author and corresponding author
  • Origin of the work (course, capstone, research program, or independent study)
  • Acknowledgments and funding sources, if any

Abstract

  • Maximum 250 words, single paragraph, no citations.
  • State the question, the approach, the main findings or argument, and the significance.
  • Provide 4–6 keywords below the abstract.

Main Text

For research articles, use the standard structure: Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion.

For reviews, hypotheses, perspectives, and concept papers, use a logical heading structure appropriate to the argument. Every manuscript should clearly state its central question early in the introduction.

Figures and Tables

  • Number figures and tables in order of citation in the text.
  • Each figure and table must have a self-contained caption.
  • Submit figures as separate image files (PNG, JPEG, or TIFF) at readable resolution.
  • Cite the source of any reproduced or adapted figure and confirm permission to reuse it.

References

  • Use author–year (APA-style) citations in the text, e.g., (Kim and Lee, 2024).
  • List references alphabetically at the end of the manuscript.
  • Include DOIs or stable URLs where available.
  • Authors are responsible for the accuracy of all references.

Example reference format:

Kim, J., Lee, H., & Park, S. (2024). Climate adaptation strategies in apple orchards. Journal of Horticultural Science, 12(3), 45–58. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxxx

AI Use Statement

Every submission must include a brief AI use statement describing whether and how artificial intelligence tools were used (e.g., literature search, language editing, data analysis, code generation). AI tools may not be listed as authors. Authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy and integrity of all content. See the Policies page for the full AI policy.

Ethical Considerations

  • The manuscript must be the authors' original work and not published or under review elsewhere.
  • All data must be reported honestly; fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism are grounds for rejection.
  • Work involving human participants or regulated organisms must follow applicable institutional rules.
  • All listed authors must have contributed meaningfully and approved the submission.

Submission Checklist

  • ☐ Manuscript file (.docx), double-spaced with numbered pages and lines
  • ☐ Separate title page with all author information
  • ☐ Abstract (≤250 words) and 4–6 keywords
  • ☐ Figures as separate files with captions (if applicable)
  • ☐ References complete and formatted
  • ☐ AI use statement included
  • ☐ First author is an undergraduate student
  • ☐ All co-authors approved the submission
